A year spent watching what blooms, and when

Seasons in Bloom began as a sketch journal — one flower drawn each week, tracking what emerged from the garden, the hedgerow, and the florist's bucket. Over twelve months those sketches became a language: a visual almanac of bloom times, botanical silhouettes, and the particular quality of light each season carries.

Each pattern pairs the traditional birth flowers of each month with the broader flora of that season, creating a system that feels both personal and universal — intimate as a birthday, expansive as the year itself.
